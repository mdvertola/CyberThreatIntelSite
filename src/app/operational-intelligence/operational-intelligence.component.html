Operational Intelligence

Twitter:

Dissemination:
  • The Twitter source has provided us with a valuable set of open source intelligence.                                                                                                             
  • We have identified key threat actors that have been highly active both now and over the last few years.
  • These actors can be shared with the healthcare industry.
    • Other players in the industry may have the capability of diving deeper and discerning more intelligence.
Course of Action:
  • Continue to monitor the hacktivist accounts we have and search for relevant tweets about data breaches.                                                                          
  • Additionally, continue to search for new accounts that crop up.
    • Many accounts go silent after a few years, and the actors are always changing.
Information for next iteration of CTI:
  • We can pass on the knowledge we have of both the actors we have discovered and the insights we have gained from these actors.
  • The next iteration of CTI processes should look to expand on the identified threat actors, as there are countless others out there.

Reddit:

Dissemination:
  • Reddit gives us a constant and diverse stream of relevant and current news related to attacks within the healthcare industry.
  • This information can be useful to all cybersecurity analysts within the healthcare industry to stay up to date with relevant news and attacks.
Course of Action:
  • Provide information to security professionals who can leverage the dataset to identify key hacking groups, targets, or technologies that may be relevant to their own organization.
Information for next iteration of CTI:
  • The next iteration of CTI should focus on expanding into other subreddits and levering the api to collect more features from a given post. This can include:
    • Links to external news sites and their content
    • Images
    • Content

URL Haus:

Dissemination:
  • Our findings require constant monitoring and should be shared with other healthcare organizations and network professionals in the industry.
  • Information sharing has been one of the most under-utilized, cost-effective, and impactful strategies organizations can engage to mitigative cyber evolving cyber threats
Course of Action:
  • We recommend deploying firewalls and to block Tor, I2P and restrict ports in order to add another layer of protection based on the URL Haus data
    • These recommendations are aligned with the strategies and goals set by MedCity which listed the tasks above as critical access control measures to protect against ransomware in the healthcare sector
Information for next iteration of CTI:
  • Our next iteration of CTI should be generated based on government agencies recommendations (e.g., FBI, CISA, etc.). We limited our focus to Trickbot & Bazar Loader because of the reported frequency of use by malicious actors in 2020.
  • Ransomware protection should be considered as a game of whack-a-mole – as one is shut down or rendered useless, another will pop up to take its place
  • Due to it’s the ever-changing ransomware landscape, our next iteration of CTI should focus on latest ransomware trends reported by trusted parties
